# Diagnosis: Inkwell Books Under-35 Lapsed-Customer Analysis

## 1. Target Cohort
**Lapsed Under-35 Readers:** Customers aged under 35 (specifically the Under-25 and 25–34 segments) who have significantly reduced their visit frequency or ceased shopping at Inkwell entirely over the past two years.

## 2. Size-of-Prize Math
To estimate the potential revenue impact of re-engaging the Under-35 segment:
1. **Total Monthly Revenue:** €372,000 (blended, §2.1).
2. **Current Under-35 Revenue Share:** 22.00% (computed from `inkwell_pos_transactions.csv`).
3. **Target Under-35 Revenue Share (Year 1 level):** 37.00% (computed from `inkwell_pos_transactions.csv`).
4. **Current Monthly Under-35 Revenue:** 22.00% * €372,000 = **€81,840**.
5. **Target Monthly Under-35 Revenue:** 37.00% * €372,000 = **€137,640**.
6. **Monthly Prize (Potential Uplift):** €137,640 - €81,840 = **€55,800 per month**.

## 3. Evidence-Backed Reasons for Lapsed Customers
1. **Branch Decay in Key Demographic Areas:** Footfall decline is highest in student/young-professional-heavy branches: Maynooth (-34%) and Dundrum Town Centre (-29.1%). These branches are essential "discovery" points for the Under-35 demographic.
2. **Loyalty Irrelevance:** The paper-based stamp card is failing. Under-35s have a lower redemption rate (11.27%) compared to the Over-35 group (14.78%), confirming the survey feedback that the card is inconvenient and unengaging for this cohort.
3. **Price Masking Volume Decline:** While AOV has increased slightly (Year 1: €17.91 → Year 2: €18.88), this is driven by pricing, not volume. Basket size has remained stagnant (Year 1: 1.89 → Year 2: 1.90), indicating that Inkwell is not successfully driving higher volume or engagement through its current offering, particularly in growing categories like Romantasy/Young Adult, which saw revenue declines similar to other categories instead of growth.

## 4. The ONE Metric to Move
**Monthly Active Under-35s (via digital ID):** The campaign must focus entirely on converting anonymous footfall into identified, opted-in email subscribers ("Library Card" holders). This digital identity will enable targeted re-engagement, bypassing the ineffective physical stamp card and allowing Inkwell to actively manage its Under-35 base.
